Weather Balloon Payload
This semester-long project was a step away from hardcore coding and into the realm of CE and EE. We designed our own PCB that contained accelerometers, altitude sensors, pressure sensors, and UV light sensors. This was all encased inside our payload structure, a hard styrofoam cube, which also contained our GoPro. Managing to keep our entire payload under 1 pound was quite an accomplishment.

So what was the overall science objective of this mission? To prove, once and for all, that the Earth is indeed flat.

Unfortunately, our hypothesis was disproven. But, on the bright side, we did capture some unforgettable images!

Maze Solver
This was my first encounter with real "algorithms", even though I had little idea of what they really were. Using Prim's algorithm, I generated perfect mazes, aka mazes that had exactly one solution. Then, I used depth-first search, breadth-first search, and A* to solve the mazes with real-time path highlighting to show how the algorithms "think". All the mazes could be loaded from and saved to .dat files. Plus, everything was packaged into a neat little GUI.
